Why They Leave

PUSH

  • Limited jobs & economic stagnation in country
  • Low wages, rural livelihood decline
  • Agriculture & traditional sectors shrinking
  • Unstable employment after graduation
  • Desire for family welfare & stability

PULL

  • Much higher wages abroad
  • Diverse job opportunities in Gulf, Malaysia, Korea etc.
  • Strong migrant & remittance networks overseas
  • Better living standards & chance for children’s future
  • Global exposure and skill development

Remittance as Share of GDP

Remittance ~27% of GDP in 2023, sustained high contribution.

Impact on Nepal

  • ✔ Supports millions of households — education, health, housing improved
  • ✔ Foreign‑exchange reserves & macroeconomic stability boosted
  • ✘ Brain‑drain: loss of skilled/young workforce (81% male, median age 28)
  • ✘ Rural labour shortage — agriculture & local industries suffer
  • ✘ Economy remains dependent on remittance — risky if global demand shifts

Migration Today: Changing Trends

More women (19%) and younger, educated Nepalese are migrating — not just traditional labour‑class workers.

Risks & Challenges

  • Global economic instability — remittance depends on foreign job markets
  • Low domestic investment — remittances often used for consumption not productivity
  • Social issues — family separation, ageing rural population, dependency culture
  • Loss of human capital — slowed local development, technical skills drain

What Can We Do?

Short-Term Measures

  • Encourage safe & legal migration channels
  • Pre‑departure training & skill certification
  • Support reintegration & job placement for returnees
  • Promote financial literacy & remittance investment

Long-Term Vision

  • Create 500,000+ quality local jobs — agriculture, MSMEs, services
  • Channel remittances into productive investment & startups
  • Strengthen technical & vocational education at home
  • Build diversified economy — reduce over‑reliance on foreign employment

Make Migration a Choice, Not a Necessity
